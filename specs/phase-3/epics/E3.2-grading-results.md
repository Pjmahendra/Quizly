# E3.2 — Grading & Results

## Purpose

Turn submitted attempts into trustworthy, reviewable results (PRD §10, §8 "Results Generated").
Objective question types (MCQ, multiple select, true/false, numerical) are graded automatically on
submission; descriptive/essay/long answers are graded by faculty in a manual evaluation workbench
with per-question marks and feedback (PRD §10). Faculty publish results; students review them
(PRD §4.4 "Review Results") and see their own performance history (PRD §4.4).

## Business Value

Grading correctness is the product's reputation: an assessment platform that mis-scores is dead on
arrival with institutions. Automating the objective portion delivers the PRD Phase 1 milestone
promise ("Objective question evaluation", PRD §18) and removes the bulk of faculty grading labor.
The manual workbench is deliberately built as the exact surface that Phase 7 AI evaluation
(PRD §11) plugs into — the "Evaluate with AI" flow adds a suggestion step to this same per-response
review UI, so investment here is investment in the flagship feature.

## Dependencies

- E3.1 — grading operates on submitted/auto-terminated attempts and their recorded
  what-the-student-saw snapshots (F3.1.2).
- Phase 2 — questions carry correct answers/answer keys and maximum marks. **Assumption:**
  question authoring captured these; if not, that gap is closed before F3.2.1.
- Code home: `modules/evaluations/` (folder-structure §4, §11 — including its `components/`
  faculty review UI); student-facing read surfaces in `app/student/`, faculty surfaces in
  `app/faculty/`.
- Within this epic: F3.2.2 and F3.2.3 depend on F3.2.1; F3.2.3 additionally depends on F3.2.2
  (publication gating on manual grading); F3.2.4 depends on F3.2.3 (history shows published
  results).

## Acceptance Criteria

- On submission (manual or auto), all objective answers are graded automatically and
  deterministically; re-running grading on the same attempt yields identical scores (idempotent,
  guidelines §4.5).
- Faculty can open any student's submission, see each subjective answer alongside the question and
  expected answer, and record marks (bounded by the question's maximum) and feedback (PRD §10).
- Results are invisible to students until faculty publish them; publication triggers the
  "Results Published" notification (PRD §14).
- After publication, a student can review their result per the visibility policy decided in
  F3.2.3's open questions, and sees their attempt history across quizzes (PRD §4.4).
- Every grading action and publication is auditable (guidelines §8.8): who graded/published what,
  when, and any subsequent mark changes.
