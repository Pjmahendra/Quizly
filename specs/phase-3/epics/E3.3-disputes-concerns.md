# E3.3 — Disputes & Concerns

## Purpose

Give students a formal channel to challenge outcomes and flag quiz problems, and faculty a place
to see and act on them. The PRD names two distinct student capabilities: "Raise Result Disputes"
and "Raise Quiz Concerns" (PRD §4.4), and a faculty notification "Student Concern Raised"
(PRD §14). Result disputes target a published result (typically a specific question's marks);
quiz concerns target the quiz itself (question errors, unclear wording, delivery problems).

**Needs clarification:** the PRD gives no resolution workflow for either capability — statuses,
who may resolve, escalation, and mark-adjustment mechanics are all open (tracked per feature).

## Business Value

Disputes are the trust escape valve: when grading is wrong (or perceived wrong), a visible,
tracked channel converts a reputational incident into a routine workflow. It also closes the loop
on manual grading quality and feeds question-quality signals back to faculty. This epic completes
the Phase 3 acceptance loop: take → grade → review → **dispute**.

## Dependencies

- E3.2 — result disputes can only be raised against published results (F3.2.3).
- E3.1 — concerns and disputes reference attempts and the recorded what-the-student-saw snapshot
  (F3.1.2) as evidence.
- Notification hook for "Student Concern Raised" (PRD §14) — email via `server/mail/` (Resend)
  and/or in-app record. **Assumption:** minimal delivery this phase; Notification Center is a
  later phase.
- Code home — **Assumption:** a new `modules/disputes/` module owning both disputes and concerns
  (standard anatomy; created under the new-domain rule, folder-structure §14), consuming
  `modules/attempts/` and `modules/evaluations/` via `index.ts` only.

|---|---|---|
| F3.3.1 | Result disputes | Medium |
| F3.3.2 | Quiz concerns | Small |

## Acceptance Criteria

- A student can raise a dispute on their own published result, optionally tied to a specific
  question, with a written justification; they can track its status and see the outcome.
- A student can raise a quiz concern distinct from a result dispute, tied to a quiz (and
  optionally a question), independent of whether results are published.
- Faculty are notified of new disputes/concerns (PRD §14) and can view, respond to, and close
  them; any mark change flows through the evaluations module and re-derives the attempt total.
- Students can only see their own disputes/concerns; faculty see those for quizzes they own;
  everything is organization-scoped (tenancy per CLAUDE.md non-negotiables).
- All raise/respond/resolve actions are recorded in the audit trail (guidelines §8.8).
