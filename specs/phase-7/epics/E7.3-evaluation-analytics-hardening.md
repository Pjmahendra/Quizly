# E7.3 — Evaluation Analytics & Hardening

## Purpose

Make AI-assisted evaluation observable, accountable, and trustworthy after it ships: the
"advanced evaluation analytics" of PRD §18 Phase 3, plus the AI action audit trail mandated by
guidelines §24 — who evaluated what, when, with which model and prompt version — recorded without
ever storing prompt or response payloads. The epic also surfaces override-rate visibility so
faculty and org admins can judge how well AI suggestions match human grading, closing the loop on
the flagship feature's quality. Analytics surfaces live in `modules/analytics/`; the trail
records through the Phase 1 audit platform via `modules/audit/` (folder-structure §4).

## Business Value

- Trust is the adoption currency of AI grading: institutions will only rely on suggestions they
  can inspect — acceptance vs. modification vs. override rates show whether the AI is actually
  helping (PRD §11 faculty-control principle, PRD §15 analytics).
- The audit trail satisfies the platform's own security posture (PRD §16 "Audit Logs";
  guidelines §8.8, §24) and answers the inevitable dispute: "who gave this grade, and was it
  AI-assisted?" — directly relevant to the student result-dispute flow (PRD §4.4).
- Model/prompt-version attribution makes grading reproducible and makes prompt regressions
  detectable when a new prompt version ships (F7.1.2).
- Failure-rate visibility tells operators whether the provider, prompts, or parser need
  attention before faculty complain.

## Dependencies

- **E7.2** — evaluation records with dispositions (accepted / modified / overridden) and
  failure states are the data this epic aggregates and audits.
- **E7.1** — model identifier and prompt version recorded per evaluation (F7.1.1, F7.1.2).
- **Phase 1** — the append-only audit log platform (F1.4.4) and analytics-view capabilities in
  the permission catalog (F1.3.x).
- Phase 3/4 analytics surfaces, where they exist, as the home for evaluation dashboards —
  extended, not forked (guidelines §25 "parallel implementation").

## Acceptance Criteria

- Every AI evaluation trigger, completion, failure, and faculty disposition writes an audit
  entry carrying actor, response identity, timestamp, model identifier, prompt version, and
  outcome — and demonstrably no prompt, answer, feedback, or reasoning payload (guidelines §24).
- Audit entries are visible where affected entities are viewed, consistent with the Phase 1
  audit platform's presentation rules (guidelines §8.8).
- Faculty and org admins (each within their capability scope) can see override-rate metrics:
  how often AI suggestions were accepted unchanged, modified, or fully overridden, and the
  score delta between suggestion and final grade, aggregated per quiz and per question.
- AI evaluation failure rates are visible to the appropriate administrative scope, split by
  failure class (provider error, timeout, malformed output).
- All analytics queries are tenant-scoped from server context and paginated/bounded
  (guidelines §1.1, §10).
- The feature passes `rules/definition-of-done.md`.
